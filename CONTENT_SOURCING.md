# Learning Content Sourcing Policy

## Standard

- Do not include factual claims that cannot be supported by a credible source.
- Prefer primary sources: government agencies, standards bodies, official specifications, and original peer-reviewed research.
- Use secondary sources only when they clearly synthesize established evidence and identify their own sources.
- Do not invent citations, examples presented as real events, statistics, regulations, clinical guidance, or product claims.
- Clearly label illustrative examples, educational synthesis, uncertainty, and limitations.
- Verify time-sensitive claims before publication and include the review date.
- Remove or qualify a claim when credible sources conflict or do not support it.

## Scope

The Learning Path is educational material for product designers. It is not medical, legal, insurance, compliance, or billing advice. Real-world decisions require validation with qualified professionals and the applicable organization, contract, regulation, standard version, and care setting.
